Working for Change

2010-2011: Joe's Freshman Year

Joe served on the Education Committee of the TCU Senate and worked on projects related to curriculum structure and professor tenure.

Joe served as a Council Chair of the Allocations Board (ALBO). Through this experience, he worked with over twenty student group leaders to help design group operating budgets for the following fiscal year.

Joe helped author and co-sponsor a Senate resolution calling for the creation of an Africana Studies Department at Tufts.

2011-2012: Joe's Sophomore Year

Joe served as the first Diversity and Community Affairs Officer of the TCU Senate. In this position, he established a strong precedent of connecting and collaborating with the Group of 6 centers, communities of color, the LGBTQ community and many others on campus.

Joe served as a member of the Senate Executive Board and met with many university administrators to discuss ongoing projects and lobbying efforts of the Senate.

Joe chaired the Senate’s Culture, Ethnicity & Community Affairs Committee (CECA). Under his direction, committee members wrote and passed resolutions on topics such as increased campus accessibility for disabled individuals, gender neutral housing, expanded alumni networks, and Hindi and Korean language course offerings.

Joe served as one of four student representatives on the student-faculty Race and Ethnicities Working Group. Through his lobbying efforts he contributed to the recent creation of an Africana Studies major and minor and an Asian-American Studies minor at Tufts.

Joe expanded the collaborative scope of Senate through joining United for Undergraduate Socioeconomic Diversity (U/FUSED), a national network of student governments dedicated to advocating for college affordability and economic equitability on campus.

2012-2013: Joe's Junior Year

Joe collaborated with different students on campus to create a new community representative position based out of the Women’s Center.

Joe attended a conference held by the Boston Intercollegiate Assembly (BIA) and networked with other schools in the Boston area to develop strategies for collaborative lobbying efforts and social opportunities.

Joe vocally opposed the Committee on Student Life’s (CSL) recent amendment to the university’s non-discrimination code that now allows groups to “reasonably depart” from the non-discrimination policy under specified conditions. Joe co-authored a resolution with other senators calling for the immediate repeal of the new policy.

Joe most recently worked to amend student government campaign finance rules to ensure more equitable and fair TCU elections. Students may vote on a referendum concerning this issue on Tuesday, April 23.

Joe continued to serve as a member of the CECA committee before leaving Tufts to study abroad in Spain.
