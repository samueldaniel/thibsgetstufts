Hey Tufts!

I’m running for TCU President because I possess the experience, ideas, and drive necessary to create substantive changes on this campus.

I believe in a Tufts where every student feels happy, safe, and welcome to pursue their passions and personal growth. Everyday, I witness our immense potential as a nursery of ideas, a foundation for active citizenship, and a vibrant community committed to diverse extracurricular, academic, and social engagement. I hope to collaborate with you over the next year as we strive for a better, more equitable, more perfect Tufts—a Tufts that is entirely within our reach. I call it, “The Tufts We Deserve”.

I am deeply passionate about this school, its students, and its mission to nurture leaders and cultivate our collective future. Over the past three years, I have had the opportunity to meet so many different people from all parts campus. Our experiences and memories have made Tufts what it is, have made Tufts matter. Whether it was performing in shows produced by the 3P’s and Torn Ticket II, singing in Concert Choir, dancing in TDC, or spinning tracks on WMFO, you and me and us have all made Tufts our school, our community, and our home.

Through the Tisch Scholars Program, I’ve been involved in the community beyond Tufts and have worked firsthand with the political and social dynamics of Medford and Somerville. I’ve volunteered at local nonprofits like LIFT Somerville and the Somerville Homeless Coalition. Through these experiences I have developed the connections and knowledge to work with our host communities, local governments, and Tufts administration to improve Tufts on- and off-campus life.

I understand the difficulties of transitioning to college, both as a freshmen and and throughout the other four years. Like many others at Tufts, I am a financial aid student. For three years I have juggled a job at the Ginn Library with the other inherent pressures of a busy class schedule and many extracurricular commitments . I have also worked as a FOCUS leader and an orientation panelist to help our newest Jumbos feel welcome on our campus. Next year, I will be living in a freshman dorm and working as an ACE Fellow to directly help freshmen through the year-long process of college adjustment.

I get Tufts, and I know how we can make it better. I understand our community as an involved student, as a student who has a life beyond TCU government, as one who faces daily challenges that implicate socioeconomic status, sexuality, alcohol policy, religion, and this school’s bureaucracy. I ask you to join me as work in partnership to change our university for the better.

Please feel free to explore the website, learn more, ask questions, and propose solutions. Email me here. I am here for you, for all of you, because I think that together we can craft a brighter future for the Tufts that we all deserve.
